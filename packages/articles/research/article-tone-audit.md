# Auditoría de Tono — 9 Artículos en luisracosta.com

Benchmarked against: `luis-writing-guide.md` + Luis's direct feedback history

**Gold standard = Luis's voice**: "Escribo como platico. Como si estuviera contando algo en una cena con gente que me importa."

**Red flags to catch**: AI-sounding language, glazing, corny hooks, copywriting frameworks, self-promotion, urgency/fear, mechanical transitions, redundancy, encabezados-truco.

---

## 1. Caballos Salvajes ✅ CLEAN

**Tone**: Perfect. This is the gold standard. "Mi abuelo me contó una historia cuando era chico." Opens with story, builds naturally, closes with "Y resulta que eso era suficiente." Zero AI smell. Written in Luis's real voice — Mexican Spanish, personal, no frameworks.

**Issues**: None.

---

## 2. El Territorio Cambió ✅ CLEAN

**Tone**: Strong. "Durante siglos, el poder se midió en tierra." Builds the tierra → capital → conocimiento → cómputo progression naturally. The closing — "Es dignidad. Es tiempo devuelto. Es la posibilidad de usar tu vida para lo que realmente importa." — lands without being corny.

**Issues**: None.

---

## 3. Por Qué Construimos Diferente ⚠️ MINOR ISSUE

**Tone**: Mostly good. Conversational, grounded in real examples (the doctor in Mérida, SETY). The section about "No cuento esto para impresionar" is a nice self-aware move.

**Issue**: The line "Hoy un médico en Mérida tiene su sitio en producción con reservaciones en línea, automatizaciones de comunicación con pacientes y su perfil de Google Business optimizado" — this reads as a case study / portfolio showcase, which brushes against Rule #2 (nunca me autopromociono). It's borderline — it says what happened without saying "I did it," but the implication is clear. Luis flagged this exact pattern with "el Doctor que es eso" — he doesn't want articles opening with stories about work done for others.

**Severity**: Low. The article doesn't open with the doctor — it opens with the agency model description. The doctor reference is mid-article as an example. But it IS the closest thing to a case study / portfolio flex across all 9 articles.

---

## 4. El Open Source Se Va a Comer al Closed Source ✅ CLEAN

**Tone**: Clean analytical piece. "El teléfono que tienes en la bolsa corre sobre Linux." Good opening — concrete, surprising for the reader. Stays balanced ("Pero sería deshonesto no decir lo que también es verdad: algunos de los mejores productos del mundo son cerrados y brillantes"). Doesn't preach. 

**Issues**: None.

---

## 5. ¿Los Gigantes Mandan? ✅ CLEAN

**Tone**: Strong analytical voice. Dense with data but presented conversationally. "Aquí está el análisis que el mercado está leyendo mal" — confident without being arrogant. The Apple historical pattern section is well-constructed. Closes with a teaser to the next essay that's subtle enough ("Eso, sin embargo, es el siguiente ensayo").

**Issue (very minor)**: "Eso, sin embargo, es el siguiente ensayo" — technically violates Rule #6 (nunca anuncio lo que viene). But it's a single sentence, not a preview paragraph, and it works as a closing beat.

**Severity**: Negligible.

---

## 6. El OS que se Construye Solo ⚠️ MINOR ISSUES

**Tone**: The most "manifesto-like" of the 9. Longer, more conceptual. The vision is ambitious and the writing stays grounded — but there are a few spots where it drifts into product pitch territory.

**Issues**:

1. **"Llamémoslo como es: un OS agéntico personalizado."** — This has a slight "let me introduce my product concept" energy. Not terrible, but the framing is more startup pitch than personal reflection.

2. **The "Las áreas que integra" section** — Listing Salud, Trabajo, Relaciones, Aprendizaje, Intereses as product features with "No como un tracker de pasos... Como un sistema que..." repeated 5 times. This is a product spec dressed as an essay. The repetitive "No como X. Como Y." structure is a framework — it violates Rule #1 (nunca frameworks de copywriting).

3. **"Para quién es esto"** — "El emprendedor joven que está construyendo algo... El CEO o director con experiencia... El profesional que opera en múltiples áreas simultáneamente" — this reads like a target market slide in a pitch deck. It's defining personas, which is a business exercise, not an essay move.

4. **"Es software premium, en el sentido más literal."** — Pricing language in an essay.

**Severity**: Medium. This article has the most "pitch deck disguised as thought piece" energy of all 9. The ideas are strong but the structure leans toward product marketing.

---

## 7. La Paradoja del Modelo Abierto ✅ CLEAN

**Tone**: Sharp analytical piece. "La narrativa de 2024 era clara: el open source estaba ganando." — Good, direct opening. The paradox framework is intellectual, not salesy. "Aquí está la parte que requiere pensar despacio" — this works as a genuine signal to the reader to pay attention, not as a trick header.

**Issues**: None. This is one of the strongest in the series. Clean analysis, no glazing, honest about the uncomfortable conclusion.

---

## 8. La Carrera de la IA es un Ciclo ⚠️ MINOR ISSUE

**Tone**: Data-heavy, analytical, confident. The Bitcoin halving parallel is a strong conceptual hook. Tables are well-used.

**Issue**: The Perplexity Computer section is the longest single-company section across all 9 articles. It reads like a feature list:
- "Multi-modelo por diseño. 19 modelos corriendo simultáneamente..."
- "Cientos de conectores. Gmail, Google Calendar, Outlook, Slack..."
- "Memoria persistente. Recuerda tus proyectos, tus preferencias..."
- "Perplexity Health... Samsung Galaxy S26... Personal Computer..."

This is 5+ paragraphs of product features for a single company. Compare to how OpenClaw and Manus are treated (1-2 paragraphs each). The imbalance makes the essay feel like a sponsored Perplexity review. Not horrible — the framing is analytical, not promotional — but the volume is notable.

**Additional**: "el agente personal para el humano productivo del futuro" — this specific line sounds like ad copy.

**Severity**: Medium-low. The analysis is honest (includes "Dónde la tesis puede estar mal" section), but the Perplexity section needs tightening to match the weight given to other players.

---

## 9. Cómo Construí Mi Sistema Operativo ✅ CLEAN (post-rewrite)

**Tone**: The rewritten version is solid. Opens with "Hay una forma de trabajar que la mayoría de la gente no ha visto todavía" — direct, confident, not glazing. The four pillars (design thinking, human-centered system design, programación, transformación digital) are stated cleanly without over-explaining. "No es magia. Es que el agente trabaja con la materia prima correcta." — good line.

**Issues**: None after the rewrite. The old version (doctor story opening) was the problem — that's been fixed.
